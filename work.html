My Community Programs

Three organizations, one trajectory: community health in San Diego built around student involvement, direct patient contact, and measurable benefit.

Aristeia Health Initiative
Founder  ·  Pediatric community health, San Diego

AHI is a student-run health research and community intervention organization focused on pediatric community health in San Diego. Every project is built around a trained student navigator embedded in a clinical or community setting, doing work that directly affects whether families get to care. The long arc is peer-reviewed publication alongside direct patient benefit. Both.

The goal chain is fixed: intervene first, collect rigorous data, publish, then use findings to advocate for systemic change. Programs that skip the data collection step are not AHI programs. They're outreach events.

What AHI isn't doing: Passive education programs, distributing handouts, or building toolkits without a direct interaction and data collection component. Every project needs a structured delivery mechanism, a theory of change, and a measurement plan from day one. A guide that sits in a waiting room is not an intervention.

Patient Navigator Service (PNS)
AHI direct service  ·  Active

The PNS is where the navigator model runs in real time. I operate it directly: receiving referrals from the Rady social work team, contacting families within 48–72 hours of discharge, running a full social determinants intake (transportation, insurance, housing, food security, language, childcare), and staying on the case until every follow-up appointment is scheduled and confirmed.

  • Full SDOH screen at every intake: a real conversation with the family
  • Insurance verification and Medi-Cal reinstatement navigation
  • Weekly check-ins through case closure; post-appointment follow-through within 48 hours
  • Escalation protocols for mental health crises, safety concerns, and family unreachability
  • Structured case documentation for every contact attempt and outcome

Building toward a formal AHI volunteer navigator program with IRB-approved outcomes data collection and a Rady partnership agreement.

If you're a student interested in a real clinical-adjacent role, or a clinician or social worker at Rady looking for a structured navigator resource, see For Students or For Partners.

San Diego Injury Prevention Program
Co-founder  ·  President of Strategy  ·  Student-run nonprofit, UC San Diego

SDIPP is a student-run nonprofit delivering injury prevention programs directly in San Diego communities. I co-founded the organization and lead as President of Strategy, overseeing program direction, community partnerships, and research output across four active committees. The work is hands-on and community-embedded.

The scope is broad: any direct, physical injury that can be prevented through education, environment design, or physical skill training. We've expanded significantly from the original fall prevention focus. Project-based work is now central to how SDIPP operates.

Fall Prevention + Mobility
Weekly hands-on fall prevention and mobility classes at senior residential facilities. Direct, physical: volunteers work alongside participants.
Pediatric Health Literacy
Health literacy and injury prevention programming in San Diego schools. Designed around the 5–14 age group where caregiver influence and school access create the strongest intervention points.
Sidewalk Safety Project
Active project addressing pedestrian injury risk in San Diego neighborhoods. Community-embedded, data-informed, focused on the built environment and behavior change.
Urban Safety + eBike Safety
Transportation safety outreach as San Diego's eBike density increases. Community health fairs and school programs addressing a genuine and growing injury risk.

What SDIPP doesn't do: Drug or overdose prevention, behavioral health as a primary focus, or programs that ask the community to come to us. The standard is embedding SDIPP in existing community routines: retirement facilities, schools, parks, health fairs. If a program doesn't involve direct physical interaction with participants and a measurement component, it isn't a SDIPP program.

National Marrow Donor Program
Intern  ·  NMDP (Be The Match)

I completed an internship with NMDP, the organization that operates the national bone marrow and stem cell registry used in transplant decisions for leukemia, aplastic anemia, sickle cell disease, and other blood disorders. The work sits at the center of pediatric hematology/oncology; transplant decisions for the conditions I research are made using this registry.

I want to continue in a research capacity. Specifically, I'm interested in outcomes research on pediatric transplant recipients: what factors affect match quality, how long-term survival varies across patient populations, and what the data shows about access disparities in who gets listed and who gets matched. These are questions where the research has real clinical stakes.
