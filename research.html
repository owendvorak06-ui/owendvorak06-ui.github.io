Clinical Research

Research

Data entry and analysis on active studies across pediatric oncology, hematology, and surgery. Four conditions, all on the hem/onc and surgical spectrum.

What this is

Where I am in the research pipeline

Clinical research at this stage is data work: entry, cleaning, and analysis on active studies run by physicians and research teams at major institutions. My role across these four projects is to handle the data carefully and learn the conditions deeply: how they're diagnosed, how treatment decisions are made, what the outcomes literature says, where the open questions are.

These aren't disconnected resume lines. Severe aplastic anemia, leukemia, and neuroblastoma all sit in pediatric hematology/oncology, the specialty I'm heading toward. The obesity surgery project is surgical outcomes research, a different part of the spectrum but the same rigor. Each project builds clinical intuition that lectures don't provide.

Active studies

Current projects

Hematology
Pediatric Severe Aplastic Anemia

SAA is a life-threatening bone marrow failure syndrome in which the immune system destroys hematopoietic stem cells. In pediatric patients, treatment decisions involve immunosuppressive therapy versus bone marrow transplant, with outcomes heavily dependent on donor match quality, disease severity, and timing.

Role: data entry and analysis

Oncology
Pediatric Leukemia

Leukemia is the most common pediatric malignancy. Research in this area addresses treatment stratification, relapse prediction, and long-term outcomes, including the late effects of chemotherapy and radiation that shape quality of life for survivors.

Role: data entry and analysis

Oncology
Neuroblastoma

Neuroblastoma is the most common extracranial solid tumor in children, arising from neural crest cells. High-risk neuroblastoma carries a poor prognosis despite aggressive multimodal therapy. Research focuses on risk stratification, treatment response, and outcomes in high-risk patients.

Role: data entry and analysis

Surgery
Pediatric Obesity Surgery

Bariatric surgery in pediatric patients is performed in a narrow subset of adolescents with severe obesity and obesity-related comorbidities. Research addresses short- and long-term surgical outcomes, metabolic changes, and the factors that predict durable benefit versus complications.

Role: data entry and analysis

Three of these four conditions (SAA, leukemia, neuroblastoma) are treated in part with bone marrow transplantation. This work connects directly to the NMDP internship and to the trajectory toward pediatric hematology/oncology. The research isn't happening in isolation from the clinical interests; it's the same field.
