About

Pediatrics is the thread.

I'm a pre-medical student at UC San Diego on track for pediatric hematology/oncology. The work I do now: patient navigation, injury prevention, clinical research. All of it oriented around the same question: what actually happens to pediatric patients outside the clinical encounter, and what can a motivated student do about it?

I co-founded the San Diego Injury Prevention Program and lead it as President of Strategy. I founded the Aristeia Health Initiative, where I'm developing student-navigator programs embedded in pediatric clinical settings at Rady Children's Hospital. I run a direct patient navigation service for families leaving Rady post-discharge. And I work on clinical research across pediatric oncology, hematology, and surgery.

The goal isn't to collect experiences. It's to build programs that outlast my involvement, generate data worth publishing, and change what's available to pediatric patients in San Diego.
